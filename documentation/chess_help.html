Chessboard Quicksheet


In standard English, there are six unique types of chessboard pieces. Each player begins a game with 16 total pieces.

Standard Chess Piece Names

  • King (Цар): The most important piece; the game ends if it is checkmated.

  • Queen (Царица): The most powerful piece, capable of moving any number of squares in any direction.

  • Rook (Топ): Also known as a castle, it moves horizontally or vertically.

  • Bishop (Офицер): Moves any number of squares diagonally.

  • Knight (Кон): Often referred to as the horse, it moves in an "L" shape and can jump over other pieces.

  • Pawn (Пешка): The most numerous piece (8 per side), often called foot soldiers.

Notation Abbreviations

In algebraic notation, these pieces are officially abbreviated by their first letter, with the exception of the knight (N) and the pawn (no letter):

  • K: King, Цар

  • Q: Queen, Царица

  • R: Rook, Топ

  • B: Bishop, Офицер

  • N: Knight, Кон

  • P: Pawn, Пешка (though not typically written in notation)

Categorization

Pieces are often grouped based on their relative value and power:

  • Major Pieces: Queen and Rook.

  • Minor Pieces: Bishop and Knight.
