Chessboard Quicksheet

In standard English, there are six unique types of chessboard pieces. Each player begins a game with 16 total pieces.

Standard Chess Piece Names

(first letter shows the algebraic notation; the pawn is often not indicated by its letter)

  • K: King (Цар): The most important piece; the game ends if it is checkmated.
  • Q: Queen (Царица): The most powerful piece, capable of moving any number of squares in any direction.
  • R: Rook (Топ): Also known as a castle, it moves horizontally or vertically.
  • B: Bishop (Офицер): Moves any number of squares diagonally.
  • N: Knight (Кон): Often referred to as the horse, it moves in an "L" shape and can jump over other pieces.
  • P: Pawn (Пешка): The most numerous piece (8 per side), often called foot soldiers.

Categorization

Pieces are often grouped based on their relative value and power:

  • Major Pieces: Queen and Rook.
  • Minor Pieces: Bishop and Knight.
